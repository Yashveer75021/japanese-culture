Spring Travel

Spring is Japan's most popular travel season, marked by the iconic cherry blossom season from late March to early May. This is the perfect time for hanami (flower viewing) parties, visiting temples surrounded by blooming gardens, and experiencing the joy of renewal that permeates Japanese culture. The weather is mild, making it ideal for walking tours and outdoor cultural activities.

Cultural Travel Tips

Travel respectfully and meaningfully with these insights into Japanese customs and cultural etiquette for visitors.

Respectful Behavior
Bow when greeting, remove shoes when required, and speak quietly in public spaces. These simple gestures show respect for Japanese customs and cultural values.
üì∏
Photography Etiquette
Always ask permission before photographing people or inside temples. Some sacred sites prohibit photography entirely to preserve the spiritual atmosphere.

Dining Customs
Say "itadakimasu" before eating and "gochisousama" after. Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice, and try to finish all food as a sign of appreciation.
üèÆ
Temple Visits
Dress modestly, observe quietly, and follow posted guidelines. Purify hands and mouth at water basins, and make small offerings if you wish to participate.
üöá
Public Transport
Keep conversations quiet, offer seats to elderly passengers, and avoid eating or drinking. The Japanese value consideration for others in shared spaces.
üí¥
Tipping Culture
Tipping is not customary in Japan and can even be considered rude. Excellent service is standard, and gratitude is expressed through respectful behavior instead.
